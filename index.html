With a mental health crisis and growing old population, there is an immediate need for developing systems that can better understand cognitive and mental health (CMH) tracking various biomarkers from fMRI, EEG, speech, movement, cognitive surveys, wearable devices, genomic and epigenomic data. One of the core technical opportunities for accelerating the computational analysis of CMH lies in multimodal (MM) machine learning (ML): learning representations that model the heterogeneity and interconnections between diverse input signals. Recently, major progress has been made in MM learning from text, speech, images, or video (Baevski et al., 2022; Ramesh et al., 2022; Ho et al., 2022), and there has also been initial success towards using MM streams to improve prediction of patient status or response to treatment in CMH applications (Zhu et al., 2018; Zhang et al., 2019; Venugopalan et al., 2021; Ehghaghi et al., 2022; Ilias et al, 2022; Shellikeri et al., 2022; Qiu et al., 2022). However, there remains computational and theoretical challenges that need to be solved in MM learning for CMH, spanning (1) collecting and sharing quality MM data for moderate and severe patients, (2) high-modality learning from many diverse and understudied signals, (3) theoretically understanding the natural way of modality connections and interactions, (4) real-world deployment concerns such as safety, robustness, interpretability, and collaboration with various stakeholders, and (5) extending MM models for extremely low resource and multilingual environments.

This workshop has three primary goals: (1) bring together experts from multiple disciplines working on MM and CMH to learn from each other, (2) encourage the development of shared goals and approaches across these communities, and (3) stimulate creation of better MM technologies for real-world CMH impact. To achieve these goals, our workshop will include a diverse lineup of invited speakers across fields associated with MM and CMH, hosting experts from ML, CV, NLP, MM learning, neuroscience, and psychology.
